This assessmnent reviews the organisation's current security risks using the ISO/IEC 27001:2022 framework as the basline. The aim is to identify the most important weaknesses and outline practical steps to redcue risk.

What stood out: 
Password practices are weak, MFA isn't enforced and there's no centralised logging or monitoring. Patch management is incosistent and third-party vendors aren't being reviewed properly. There's also no formal incident respone plan, which makes it harder for the business to react quickly during a security event.

Overall risk level:
Moderate to High - Mainly due to gaps in access control, monitoring and general governance.

Whats needs to happen next:
1. Roll out MFA accross all systems.
2. Set up centralised logging and monitoring.
3. Create a proper patching proccess and stick to it.
4. Review and assess key vendors at least once a year.
5. Build and test and incident response plan so the team knows what to during an incident.

This summary gives leadership a clear picture of the organisation's current risk posture and the steps that will strenghten its security moving forward.
