ISO 27001 Risk Assessment Scope

This risk assessment focuses on the parts of the organisation that are most important for day‑to‑day operations and most likely to be affected by security issues. The scope includes the internal network, user accounts, core business applications, cloud services currently in use, and the data these systems store or process. These areas were chosen because they represent the highest concentration of operational and security risk.

The assessment covers the organisation’s access control practices, patching and update processes, logging and monitoring, vendor and third‑party relationships, and incident response readiness. These components form the backbone of ISO 27001 and have a direct impact on the organisation’s ability to prevent, detect, and respond to security incidents. The review also considers policies, procedures, and general governance, since these influence how consistently security controls are applied.

Several areas are intentionally out of scope. Physical security, HR processes, and financial systems were not included in this assessment, as they fall under separate internal reviews. Legacy systems that are no longer in active use were also excluded. These exclusions help keep the assessment focused on the systems and processes that matter most to current operations.

Overall, the scope is designed to be realistic for a small‑to‑medium organisation preparing for ISO 27001 alignment. It concentrates on the systems and processes that directly support business operations and where improvements will have the biggest impact on reducing risk.
