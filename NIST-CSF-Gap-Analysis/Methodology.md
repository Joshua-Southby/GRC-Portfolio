ISO 27001 Risk Assessment Methodology

This assessment was carried out using the ISO/IEC 27001:2022 framework as the main reference point. The goal was to understand the organisation’s current security posture, identify weaknesses, and determine how those weaknesses affect overall risk. The approach was kept simple and practical, focusing on the areas that matter most to day‑to‑day operations.

The assessment began by reviewing the organisation’s existing documentation, including any available policies, procedures, and system information. This helped build a basic understanding of how security is currently managed. Discussions with staff were used to clarify how certain processes work in practice, especially around access control, patching, monitoring, and incident handling. These conversations helped highlight gaps between what is written down and what actually happens.

Risks were evaluated using a straightforward scoring method based on likelihood and impact. Likelihood reflects how easily a threat could occur, while impact reflects the potential damage to the organisation if it did. Combining these two factors provided a simple way to prioritise which risks need attention first. This method keeps the assessment easy to understand while still aligning with ISO 27001 expectations.

The review also compared the organisation’s current controls against relevant ISO 27001 requirements. This made it easier to see where controls were missing, incomplete, or not consistently applied. Areas like access control, logging, patching, vendor management, and incident response were examined closely because they tend to have the biggest influence on overall risk.

Overall, the methodology focuses on clarity and practicality. It avoids unnecessary complexity and instead aims to give the organisation a clear picture of where it stands and what steps will make the biggest difference. The result is a realistic assessment that supports better decision‑making and helps guide future improvements.
